Hlawga National Park

Hlawga Park is an open zoo in Myanmar's Yangon Region, covering 6.23 km2 (2.41 sq mi) that was established in 1982.[1] It was created to protect evergreen, mixed deciduous and swamp forest and for environmental education. Located in Mingaladon Township,

Hlawga National Park, being a popular destination for day-visits and picnics, especially due to its proximity to Yangon, is likely affected by the broader challenges of waste management in Myanmar.

While specific, recent reports focused solely on the park's litter problem are limited, the search results indicate a context of widespread issues.

Hlawga – Issues

Infrastructure Deficiencies

There's a recognized lack of capacity and infrastructure to handle growing waste from urbanization.

Improper Disposal

Uncollected waste is often dumped in streets, water bodies, or burned openly, causing pollution.

High Visitor Traffic

Picnic and weekend visitors create large amounts of waste in picnic spots and roads.

Lampi National Marine Park

Some of the biggest challenges in conserving Lampi National Park include conflicts between conservation and development, human population pressures and illegal activities such as blast fishing.

Despite conservation efforts, threats like habitat loss and overfishing continue to be major issues.

Lampi – Issues

Biodiversity Under Pressure

The park faces threats from agriculture, wood harvesting, economic development and illegal activities.

Overfishing & Illegal Fishing

Depleted fish stocks are driving fishermen to resort to illegal methods like blast fishing.

Habitat Loss

Increasing human activity has led to habitat destruction in key areas.

Hkakaborazi National Park

Hkakabo Razi National Park in Kachin State, Myanmar, is the country's largest national park, encompassing Southeast Asia's highest peak, Mt. Hkakabo Razi, and serving as a vital, biodiverse Himalayan ecosystem with rich forests, unique orchids, and rare wildlife, accessible via Putao for trekkers and researchers, though challenges exist with illegal logging and mining. Hkakaborazi National Park is one of the most biodiverse areas in Myanmar but faces challenges such as limited access, environmental issues, and the impacts of human activity.

Hkakaborazi – Issues

Conservation Challenges

Conservation is difficult due to the remote location, harsh weather and lack of resources.

Human Pressures

Increasing population growth, tourism and resource extraction threaten ecosystems.

Biodiversity Risks

Many species are at risk due to climate change and human-caused habitat loss.
