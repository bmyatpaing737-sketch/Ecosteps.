Visitor Guide

Eco Park Rules & Guidelines

Stick to the walking path

Remaining on designated paths helps protect wildlife habitats and prevents damage to growing plants and soil.

The technique involves using the stick to offload stress from joints, push off for propulsion, and create a wider base for steadiness, similar to outriggers for a canoe, making hikes more comfortable and longer.

Supervise children & pets

Keep children and pets close to ensure safety, avoid disturbing wildlife, and help maintain a peaceful environment.

Supervising children and pets together is crucial for safety, requiring constant, close adult presence to prevent accidents and teach gentle interaction,

Be respectful to other visitors

Keep noise low and share pathways politely so everyone can enjoy a calm and beautiful park experience.

To be respectful to other visitors, be mindful of shared spaces, lower your voice, respect queues and personal space, learn basic greetings in the local language, dress modestly (especially at religious sites), avoid intrusive questions or gossip, and always ask before taking or touching things.

Don’t pick flowers or plants

Plants play an important role in the ecosystem. Picking them harms the natural balance and reduces biodiversity.

Repeated or large-scale removal can reduce pollinator food and local abundance. Pollinators: Flowers are food/nectar sources. Picking during peak bloom can reduce resources for bees, butterflies, and other pollinators--especially in urban or degraded habitats where floral resources are scarce.

Dispose of trash properly

Use trash and recycling bins to keep the environment clean and protect wildlife from harmful waste.

To dispose of trash properly, sort waste into general garbage, recyclables, and organics (compost), using designated bins.

Sustainable Practices

Reduce plastic use

Use reusable bags, bottles, and containers to reduce single-use plastic waste and protect the environment.

Recycle when possible

Sort and recycle paper, plastic, glass, and metals to reduce landfill waste and conserve natural resources.

Carry reusable water bottles

Reusable bottles reduce plastic use and save money. Refill them instead of buying disposable bottles.

Participate in eco-friendly actions

Join clean-ups, plant trees, and support environmental programs to help maintain a healthier planet.

Ecosteps
